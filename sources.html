"About Us" Impossible Project US. N.p., n.d. Web. 06 Nov. 2016.
This site provided all the information I needed to learn about The Impossible Project, a company that purchased the last Polaroid Company. Upon purchasing it, they realized there were no instructions on how to make the film or the Polaroid cameras, so the company immediately got to work on reinventing the process. Within a year they were successful and were helping bring back the Polaroid Camera.

McGrath, Jane. "How Ink-free Mobile Photo Printers Work." HowStuffWorks. N.p., 24 June 2008. Web. 06 Nov. 2016.
This site taught me about how ZINK, or zero ink technology works. This technology is what made Polaroid’s digital PoGo camera possible, as well as their portable printer. The process is very similar to that of the classic film with the reaction taking place in the film itself.

"Polaroid." Polaroid. N.p., 2014. Web. 05 Nov. 2016.
This was by far the most helpful site. They had a timeline of the company which I found extremely useful up until about 1999, when the timeline stops for 9 years. These were the years in which the company went bankrupt. This site was also helpful in showing me what Polaroid has done in recent times.

Zhang, Michael. "Casio QV-10: The First Digital Camera That Offered an LCD Screen and Live View." PetaPixel. N.p., 05 Sept. 2012. Web. 06 Nov. 2016.
I wanted to try to pinpoint the exact moment in history that sent the Polaroid Camera into obsolescence and found my answer on this site. This page gave helpful information on the first digital camera with an LCD screen. Now, while people couldn’t hold their picture right after they took it, they could see it, and retake it if necessary.
